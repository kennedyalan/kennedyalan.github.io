## Design Context

### Users
Two primary audiences: freelance clients (business owners who want web solutions) and tech recruiters/employers looking to hire a full-stack developer. Both visit with intent — they're evaluating Alan as a professional, not casually browsing. They need to quickly assess competence, credibility, and personality.

### Brand Personality
Creative, confident, distinctive. Alan is a full-stack developer with +2 years experience, based in Corrientes, Argentina, open to work. The interface should make him memorable — not just another dev portfolio in the pile. Tone: direct, warm, technically capable.

### Aesthetic Direction
Bolder and more distinctive. The current design leans on a popular Tailwind portfolio template pattern with yellow accents on dark/light toggle. Goal is to push past that into something genuinely memorable. Yellow accent is a starting point worth keeping but needs more intentional use. Anti-reference: the sea of clone dev portfolios that all feel the same (gray/dark, yellow or cyan accent, timeline experience, card grid projects).

### Design Principles
1. **Memorable over safe** — every design decision should have a point of view, not just be "nice"
2. **Confidence through specificity** — precise typography, deliberate spacing, not generic defaults
3. **Dual audience clarity** — clients need to feel "he can build my business thing", recruiters need to feel "he knows what he's doing technically"
4. **Spanish-first voice** — the copy is in Spanish and should feel natural and human, not translated-feeling
5. **Motion with purpose** — interactions should reinforce personality, not just add movement
